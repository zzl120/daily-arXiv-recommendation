The root cause is the ``Intent-Query Gap'', where users' intent cannot be captured by a simple text query. While interactive retrieval shifts the paradigm from static matching to multi-turn dialogue, it exposes a critical : the lack of an optimal policy for and to ask. Existing solutions relying on complex agents or rigid heuristics~ often lack transparency and efficiency. To bridge this gap, we propose , a lightweight, training-free framework powered by an information-theoretic engine. By dynamically diagnosing retrieval uncertainty via and entropy, ADEPT adaptively arbitrates between multi-granularity and strategies. This offers an interpretable, zero-shot solution to the intent disambiguation problem. Experiments on MAFW~ and MER2024~ confirm that ADEPT significantly outperforms non-interactive and general-purpose Video-LLM baselines. The core contributions of this research are as follows: We propose the ADEPT framework, featuring a core entropy-driven engine that dynamically adapts its dialogue strategy in a zero-shot manner.
