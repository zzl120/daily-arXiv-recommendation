Recent progresses~ have shown the effectiveness of generative retrieval paradigm. By reformulating retrieval task as a conditional sequence generation problem, this approach directly generates target items from the entire corpus. Nevertheless, we observe that deploying such a method in large-scale industrial recommender systems remains challenging. First, to handle high-volume traffic and ensure user experience, industrial systems typically employ the pagination request mechanism. Within each paginated request, a user might exhibit multiple positive interactions , leading to identical model inputs yet multiple valid output in generative task following the vanilla NTP paradigm. Second, the long user historical behavior sequences can lead to substantial computational overhead and increased online inference latency. Third, naively aligning the generative model with personalized objectives could result in reward hacking and performance degradation. Traditional large-scale industrial recommendation systems predominantly adopt cascade architectures consisting of retrieval, pre-ranking, ranking, and re-ranking stages. While effective, this paradigm suffers from fundamental limitations, including fragmented efficiency–accuracy trade-offs~ and misaligned optimization objectives across stages. Although recent approaches have attempted to mitigate these issues through joint or cascading optimization~, such methods often introduce strong inter-stage dependencies and complexity while yielding diminishing returns. To address these challenges, Generative Retrieval has emerged as a promising paradigm. Redefining recommendation as sequence-to-sequence generation, studies optimize backbones: HSTU boosts efficiency via gated linear recurrence instead of attention, MTGR builds an industrial framework on it to balance scalability and precision, and OneTrans enhances generalization through multi-task learning and knowledge transfer. Treating items as tokens causes large vocabularies and severe cold-start problems. To address this, Semantic IDs via vector quantization are explored: TIGER uses RQ-VAE for cold-start transfer, LETTER optimizes codebooks end-to-end, and OneRec adopts iterative RQ K-means for efficient hierarchical IDs. Recent work pursues unified representations to integrate semantic tokens’ generalization and atomic IDs’ specificity. This paper presents , a generative retrival model with preference alignment. To address industrial challenges, we synergize Multimodal Semantic IDs and an Token Merger for efficient representation learning, while employing Page-wise Generative SFT and GRPO-SR to ensure alignment with hierarchical business objectives. Large-scale deployment confirms that this differentiable paradigm significantly outperforms traditional multi-stage pipelines, validating its potential as a scalable solution for next-generation recommendation. We will investigate the reasoning ability of such framework for future work.
