However, scaling to these lengths presents a dual challenge: a representation bottleneck caused by traditional item indexing and a computational bottleneck inherent in Transformer architectures. Sequential recommendation has evolved from early RNNs to self-attention mechanisms , but the quadratic complexity of Transformers necessitated two-stage industrial solutions. Beyond target-aware attention , methods scaling to longer histories employ either heuristic filtering or hierarchical clustering . However, these decoupled strategies either incur information loss by discarding signals or require complex offline pre-computation. Consequently, recent efforts have pivoted toward optimizing architectures to model full sequences directly in an end-to-end manner. Following the early validation of Transformers in BST , recent industrial powerhouses have introduced sophisticated optimizations to push the sequence length boundaries. For instance, ByteDance introduced LONGER to scale sequence length and HyFormer to unify sequential behaviors with non-sequential interactions. Simultaneously, Meta pushed the boundaries of generative and representation learning with DV365 , HSTU and VISTA for universal user sequence representation. Parallel to architectural scaling, item representation is undergoing a paradigm shift. Early solutions like TDM and Deep Retrieval relied on hashed IDs, presenting two major flaws: Semantic fragmentation: Treating IDs as orthogonal tokens forces models to "memorize" interactions, leading to poor generalization on cold-start content. Storage and sparsity: Hashed IDs create prohibitively large embedding tables that scale unboundedly with corpus size. Recently, the field has evolved towards content-native Semantic Identifiers via RQ-VAE . While works like PLUM and TRM explore hierarchical SIDs, their synergy with ultra-long sequence modeling remains under-explored. In this work, we present a framework that bridges this gap by synergizing the generalization power of hierarchical SIDs with a Global-Aware Compressed Transformer. By adopting content-native SIDs, we enhance the model's generalization from semantic representation. Concurrently, we leverage a parameter-free temporal folding strategy that explicitly trades temporal resolution for feature dimensionality. This structural reorganization allows us to scale user histories to thousands of interactions while drastically reducing memory footprint, effectively supporting production deployment. To summarize, our main contributions are as follows: We present a large-scale industrial deployment of SIDs based on RQ-VAE within a billion-user recommendation system. By implementing a Depth-Truncated Bi-gram strategy, we effectively resolve the vocabulary and sparsity bottlenecks inherent in traditional Video IDs. This approach decouples embedding storage from corpus size while significantly improving generalization to cold-start content. We design a Global-Aware Compressed Transformer that leverages non-parametric temporal folding to condense sequence length. This achieves a performance-preserving reduction in computational complexity while simultaneously enhancing representational expressivity. By stacking windows into super-tokens, the model expands the feature dimension to capture complex local interactions within ultra-long user sequences. We provide rigorous offline and online evaluations on a major short-video platform serving billions of users. Our results demonstrate a substantial reduction in both peak memory and computational cost within the sequence modeling component. These efficiency gains allow us to scale sequence lengths, yielding significant online gains in satisfied watch time. We present a high-performance sequence modeling framework deployed on a global short-video platform serving billions of active users. The system is designed to capture user interests over horizons spanning thousands of interactions while adhering to strict millisecond-level inference latency constraints. This prevents feature misalignment between long-term historical signals and real-time ranking features. To transcend the sparsity and cold-start limitations of traditional orthogonal Video IDs, we transition to a content-native paradigm utilizing the Semantic Identifier infrastructure . Our system ingests SIDs generated via an upstream Residual Quantized VAE framework. This infrastructure projects high-dimensional, multi-modal content embeddings into a discrete, hierarchical latent space.
