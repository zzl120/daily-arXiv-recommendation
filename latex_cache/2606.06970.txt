With the rapid growth of mobile Internet technologies, live streaming has become a key form of digital media, enabling real-time broadcasting by streamers and user-streamer interaction. Alongside short videos and digital news, it holds rising societal and commercial importance, driving research interest in live streaming~recommendation~. Recent studies have explored various aspects of live streaming recommendation, including capturing interaction patterns between users and streamers~, representing the multimodal content of live rooms~, and modeling dynamic contextual factors inherent to live streaming. Representative examples include highlight detection~ and forecasting future live streaming content~. The rise of large language models ~ has driven advances in generative recommendation, applied to retrieval, pre-ranking, and ranking stages with promising industrial results. Most work focuses on the stage~, where items are encoded as semantic IDs and retrieval is reformulated as a next-token prediction task, generating candidate SIDs~. Others improve retrieval via multi-step reasoning, either explicitly or implicitly . In the stage~, LLM-inspired methods boost performance by scaling parameters and enhancing cross-feature interaction, as in MTGR~ and RankMixer~. Recently, generative recommendation~ has emerged. Unlike traditional multi-stage cascades constrained by earlier-stage outputs, these models jointly optimize the entire pipeline by combining large-scale generative modeling with reward signals, exemplified by systems such as OneRec~, OneSearch~, and GPR~. Unlike existing static SID methods, we employ dynamic SIDs to model real-time information in live streaming. Live streaming integrates real-time content delivery with rich user–streamer interactions~, posing challenges distinct from short video recommendation~. Key characteristics include: ~timeliness: live rooms exist only during broadcasts; ~dynamics: content and user preferences evolve through real-time interactions; and ~multi-entity relationships: involving users, streamers, live rooms, and often products in e-commerce~, limiting the effectiveness of static feedback approaches. Research spans streaming data modeling and context adaptation. Data modeling addresses repeated behaviors, multimodal fusion of video, audio, and text~, and cross domain transfer from short video signals to mitigate sparsity problem~. Context adaptation captures temporal and contextual changes through highlight detection~, and forecasting streamer actions~. Recent trends focus on real-time, multimodal, and LLM-enhanced systems~ that jointly optimize accuracy and latency. In this work, we enhance live streaming recommendation by leveraging generative recommendation techniques.
