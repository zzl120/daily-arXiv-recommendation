In a typical RAG pipeline, when a user submits a query , the retriever first encodes it into an embedding vector using a text encoder and then retrieves the most similar texts from an external knowledge database. These retrieved texts are subsequently provided as context to the LLM to guide and enhance the response generation process. RAG has been widely adopted in various real-world applications due to its strengths in knowledge augmentation and high-quality generation, with prominent examples including ChatGPT~, Microsoft Bing Chat~, and Google Search AI~. However, the integration of external knowledge databases further exacerbates concerns regarding the security of LLMs~. Recent studies have shown that injecting malicious content into retrieved texts can steer the LLM to generate responses aligned with the attacker’s intent , thereby posing a serious threat to the reliability and security of RAG systems~. To counter attacks induced by poisoned texts, AstuteRAG~ and TrustRAG~ introduce detection-based defenses that leverage the internal knowledge of LLMs to identify and filter maliciously retrieved content. However, their effectiveness is limited in scenarios where the LLM lacks sufficient knowledge to recognize the poisoned inputs. Moreover, existing methods~ lack a theoretical framework for certifying or quantifying the robustness of RAG systems, leaving their reliability under adversarial conditions largely unverified. RobustRAG~ guarantees robustness for LLM outputs but incurs high computational overhead by requiring multiple LLM generations. In this work, we propose PRA-RAG, a rovably obust retrieval ggregation algorithm for systems, designed to mitigate the risk of poisoning attacks at the retrieval stage. During retrieval, we intentionally expand the candidate set to increase informational diversity, which enhances the system’s resilience to poisoned content by reducing the influence of any single malicious text. The aggregation process consists of three key steps. , multiple subsets of the retrieved texts are sampled to form diverse candidate combinations, thereby constraining the influence of poisoned content within a controllable range. , Each combination is encoded into an embedding vector. In this geometric space, we identify a that encloses more than half of the combinations and take its center as the selected robust subset. , a weighted average of the selected subset’s embeddings yields a robust representation that captures consensus and reduces outlier influence. This black-box constraint poses a significant challenge to PRA-RAG, which relies on embedding-level manipulation and aggregation. To address this limitation, we design a surrogate-based pipeline that enables embedding extraction, robust aggregation, and downstream generation in a fully black-box-compatible manner. Specifically, we introduce an open-source LLM as a surrogate model to approximate the latent representation space of the target black-box model. Given a set of retrieved contexts, we first map each text sequence into its corresponding token-level embedding sequence using the surrogate model’s embedding layer . Since different contexts may have varying lengths, we apply linear interpolation to align all embedding sequences to a fixed length $L_{}$, ensuring positional consistency for subsequent aggregation. Next, we perform the aforementioned robust aggregation over the aligned embedding sequences, resulting in a set of robust embedding sequences. Subsequently, we apply softmax-weighted aggregation over the Top-$k$ relevance scores to obtain the fused embedding $_{}$, reducing noise while preserving key semantics. To bridge the gap between the continuous embedding space and the discrete input space required by the black-box model, we further design a decoding step that reconstructs pseudo-text from the aggregated embeddings. This is achieved via a token-level latent reconstruction process: for each position, we compute the cosine similarity between the fused embedding $_{}$ and the surrogate model’s vocabulary embedding matrix $ ^{V D}$, and select the token with the highest similarity . The resulting sequence of token IDs is then decoded into natural language text using the surrogate tokenizer. Finally, the reconstructed pseudo-text is fed into the black-box LLM as input for response generation. In this way, our framework preserves the benefits of embedding-level robust aggregation while remaining fully compatible with black-box LLMs. This design not only avoids direct reliance on inaccessible internal states but also provides a flexible and practical solution for extending PRA-RAG to real-world API-based systems.
