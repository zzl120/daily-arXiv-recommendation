To ensure reproducibility, we detail the model architecture, training configuration, and hyperparameters used in our experiments. All models are implemented in TensorFlow and trained on a high-performance computing cluster. We adopt a shared encoder architecture for both the Teacher and the Student to extract representations from user behavior sequences and candidate items. The encoder is a Transformer-based architecture consisting of $N_{enc}=4$ layers. It employs Multi-Head Self-Attention with $H=3$ heads, a hidden dimension of $d_{model}=256$, and an inner feed-forward dimension of $d_{ffn}=256$. The input sequence length is set to $L=30$, corresponding to the re-ranking candidate list size. The Teacher employs a Transformer Decoder architecture initialized with $N_{teacher}=4$ layers. The Student is a lightweight Transformer Decoder consisting of only $N_{student}=1$ layer. It incorporates both causal self-attention and cross-attention mechanisms to interact with the encoder outputs efficiently. This shallow architecture significantly reduces inference latency while maintaining representation capability through distillation.
