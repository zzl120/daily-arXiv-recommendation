The dominant retrieval paradigm ranks each candidate passage $c$ by $$, a score that depends only on the original query. We presented , a training-free multi-hop retrieval method that uses a bridge-conditioned tripartite judge to disambiguate parallel reasoning chains at the second hop. The motivating observation is that the hop-1 bridge passage carries entity and relational content that is absent from the original query---content that, on parallel-chain queries, is critical for identifying the active reasoning chain.
