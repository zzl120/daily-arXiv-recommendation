The model then begins the autoregressive prediction of the masked tokens, using a constrained beam search. Crucially, during the prediction process, we adopt a constrained decoding strategy based on a pre-constructed prefix tree rather than an unconstrained beam search. Prior SID-based diffusion models typically treat all interacted items with a uniform diffusion mechanism, which overlooks the co-existence phenomenon of preference consistency and interest deviations inherent in the user interaction history. By explicitly disentangling the user preference into period preference and point preference, our TDPM fundamentally addresses this limitation. The period preference encapsulates the stable user profile accumulated over time. The point preference, in contrast, sensitively captures the user transient short-term interests. Unlike prior diffusion-based recommenders that adopt the forward diffusion process of standard diffusion model, TDPM dynamically modulates the masking probability of each interacted item according to the weighted fusion of the period preference and the point preference. This design fundamentally reconciles the uniform forward diffusion process of standard diffusion model with the inherently non-uniform preference distribution in the user interaction history. The consistent superiority of our proposed TDPM on all three datasets underscores the effectiveness of the time-aware token diffusion based on preference disentanglement. In this paper, we bridge the gap between the uniform forward diffusion process of standard diffusion model and the non-uniform user preference distribution in sequential recommendation by and . Specifically, we first disentangle user preference from a temporal perspective into period preference and point preference, explicitly modeling both enduring preference consistency and abrupt interest deviations. Building upon this disentangled preference modeling, we propose the time-aware token diffusion, realized as time-aware token masking that dynamically modulates the masking probability of each interacted item according to the disentangled period preference and point preference, fundamentally departing from the indiscriminate uniform diffusion adopted by prior SID-based diffusion models. Extensive experiments on three public real-world datasets demonstrate the necessity and superiority of the proposed time-aware token diffusion for SID-based diffusion models.
