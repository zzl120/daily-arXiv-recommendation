In practical applications, the demand for cross-domain collaboration is growing between different independent entities, such as e-commerce platforms and fresh delivery apps, or offline supermarkets and online shopping guides. Specifically, we assume an attacker can intercept the uploaded item embeddings, and the attacker is tend to infer other items that are interacted by the same user. For any intercepted item, we first retrieve the most similar Top-items via computing the similarities between it and all the other items in the item set. The integration of a fine-grained semantic transfer module and a multimodal fusion mechanism enables effective combination of global semantics with local personalized features. Nevertheless, the framework exhibits certain limitations: the federated clustering process and multi-round communication protocol introduce substantial communication overhead, and the current model relies on the assumption of static data distributions, thus lacking adaptability to dynamic changes in item repositories and user preferences. Future research should prioritize the development of efficient communication compression algorithms and asynchronous update mechanisms to enhance the practicality and scalability of the framework, while also improving its capability to operate in dynamically evolving environments.
