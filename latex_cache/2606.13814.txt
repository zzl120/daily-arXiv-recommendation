Each retrieval requires a call to an external system , followed by a call to the LLM. Both incur time, monetary, and energy costs, and inference is now the dominant contributor to large-scale LLM energy consumption~. Reducing the average number of calls per query therefore lowers all three costs simultaneously. A common failure mode of iterative retrieval is overspending: the agent continues retrieving after the model has already converged on an answer, incurring calls that change neither the prediction nor the supporting evidence. The standard remedy is to learn a stopping policy from labeled trajectories. Adaptive-RAG~ trains a classifier to route questions to no-retrieval, single-step, or multi-step strategies. Stop-RAG~ learns a value head over retrieval trajectories, while Self-RAG~ trains the language model to emit retrieve and critique tokens. All three place the stopping decision in a learned component that is less transparent and typically requires retraining for new models or tasks. This paper studies the stopping decision in isolation. We present , a one-line predicate that stops the loop when the model repeats its previous-round normalized answer the calibrated probability of being correct exceeds $0.25$. TASR uses no learned classifier or value head, and the threshold is fixed across all twenty-four configurations we evaluate. It serves as a drop-in alternative to fixed call budgets and learned controllers ~ transferring across models and datasets without retraining. Our contributions can be summarized as follows: ~a one-line predicate that beats fixed-$k{=}3$ by $+3.42$ macro F1 on a $3{ }2$ distractor grid at matched cost; ~external validation on nine open-domain BM25 cells confirming that the pattern generalizes; ~full auditability using two scalars per round; ~a grounded analysis showing that Gemma's ${ }2 $ overconfident raw logit margins are corrected by isotonic calibration, explaining why TASR's largest gains appear on the weakest model; and ~a signal-quality diagnostic showing that verbalized confidence collapses on RLHF-tuned models while the logit margin preserves $44 $ better class-conditional separation. Self-Ask~ decomposes a complex question into follow-up sub-questions, retrieving evidence for each. FLARE~ predicts the next sentence and triggers retrieval whenever it contains a low-confidence token, then regenerates; this is the closest prior signal to the calibrated logit margin we use, but FLARE applies it within a single generation pass and uses it to decide , not when to stop. None of these works isolates the stopping decision as a standalone component. Adaptive-RAG~ trains a smaller language model on automatically derived complexity labels to classify each incoming question as no-retrieval, single-step, or multi-step, and applies the resulting strategy uniformly to the whole question. Self-RAG~ trains the generator language model itself to emit retrieve and critique special tokens, using a critic model supervised by GPT-4 to label training data. Stop-RAG~ trains a value head on full retrieval trajectories to decide when to halt. All three approaches tie the stopping decision to a trained component and require retraining for each new model or task.
